# German Construction Materials Market — Infographic (Bauhaus Style)

## Creative Brief for Luma Labs

### Topic & Scope

A market overview infographic visualizing the German construction materials landscape: market size, price volatility, key materials, and the pain of uncontrolled procurement costs. Bold Bauhaus visual language transforms dry market data into a striking, poster-like infographic. Educational and impactful — designed to stop scrolling and demand attention.

### One Big Takeaway

German construction material prices have seen unprecedented volatility since 2021 — steel prices swung over 80% in a single year. Companies without real-time price intelligence are flying blind in a EUR 500+ billion market.

### Target Audience

Cold leads and SEO/content visitors: German construction decision-makers, industry analysts, trade journalists. People searching for "Baustoffpreise Entwicklung" or "Materialkosten Baubranche." They want credible data presented in a visually memorable way.

### Narrative Flow

A modular poster-style layout:

1. **THE MARKET** — Massive bold typography: "500+ MRD. EUR" in red (#C1292E) on white. Below: geometric cityscape silhouette (rectangles and triangles forming buildings and cranes in black). Source line: "Destatis / Hauptverband der Deutschen Bauindustrie"

2. **THE VOLATILITY** — Bold bar chart using Bauhaus color blocks. Each material = a colored bar:
   - Red bars: Stahl (steel) — tallest, showing +80%
   - Yellow bars: Holz (lumber) — volatile
   - Black bars: Beton (concrete) — steady rise
   - Terracotta bars: Kupfer (copper) — fluctuation
   Label in bold type: "PREISVOLATILITÄT AUF REKORDNIVEAU"

3. **THE PAIN** — Three geometric icons in circles:
   - Red circle: clock = "40% Einkaufszeit für Recherche"
   - Yellow circle: Euro = "40-60% Materialkosten"
   - Black circle: warning = "~400,000 Unternehmen ohne Monitoring"

4. **THE SOLUTION** — Yellow banner strip at bottom with BauPreis AI logo: "ECHTZEITDATEN. KI-PROGNOSEN. KOSTENKONTROLLE."

### Key Stats to Include

- EUR 500+ billion construction volume in Germany (Source: Destatis, Bauvolumenrechnung)
- ~400,000 construction companies in Germany (Source: Destatis, Unternehmensregister)
- Steel prices rose ~80% in 2021 (Source: Destatis, Erzeugerpreisindex)
- Material costs represent 40-60% of total project costs (Source: Hauptverband der Deutschen Bauindustrie)
- ifo Business Climate Index for construction has shown increasing uncertainty (Source: ifo Institut)

### Visual Style

Bold Bauhaus poster aesthetic. Red (#C1292E), black (#1A1A1A), golden yellow (#F5C518), white (#FFFFFF), terracotta (#BC8279). Flat color fields — no gradients, no shadows, no 3D. Thick black outlines on all shapes. Charts use solid color blocks instead of thin lines. Typography: oversized bold sans-serif for numbers, medium weight for labels. Geometric icons (circles, squares, triangles) represent concepts. Grid-based modular composition. Inspired by Bauhaus posters, Russian Constructivism, and Swiss International Style. Each data point is a visual statement.

### Layout Concept

16:9 horizontal. Divided into 4 modular blocks in a 2x2 grid. Top-left: massive EUR number on white. Top-right: geometric bar chart on black background. Bottom-left: three pain icons in circles on red background. Bottom-right: BauPreis AI solution strip on yellow background. Bold black divider lines between blocks. Source citations integrated into each block in small monospace type. Overall feel: a Bauhaus exhibition poster that also communicates market intelligence.

### Format

16:9 horizontal — website content section / blog / SEO landing page

### Tone

Bold and confrontational. "These numbers demand your attention." Strong visual impact, industrial confidence, graphic clarity. Like a German trade union poster meets modern data journalism — impossible to ignore.
